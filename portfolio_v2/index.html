IRON FOREST

is a 2D platformer game about destroying steampunk machines using a bow and arrows.

The game currently has a 'defend the castle' gameplay with waves of enemies invading the forest that the protagonist has to defeat. Yet the game was imagined as a full adventure with puzzle elements centered around a core mechanics of shooting arrows.

It was built with a custom engine in C++ and the SFML library, mostly for handling graphics. All of the art including images, animated sprites, music and sound effects are original.

All the code is available on GitHub!

PLAN BLOCKS

is a virtual reality game that places you in the middle of an attacked medieval village!

Use your body motions to throw axes, block attacks with a shield and evade incoming projectiles. Achieve complete immersion, free from any handheld controllers, with the use of a VR headset and a body sensor.

The project was developed as part of the Advanced Graphics course at KTH and was presented at Forskar Fredag and Comicon in Stockholm, Sweden where it got positive feedback from people of all ages.

SURVIVEVR

is a survival virtual reality game, developed for the HTC Vive platform.

The game places the player in a medium sized graveyard environment. To survive the approaching waves of enemies, the player uses both long and close range weapons - a bow and a sword. Randomly spawning checkpoints with all sorts of power ups make the game more interesting.

Oh, and there's some gesture recognitions for spell casting too!

The project was developed at EPFL in Lausanne, Switzerland in May, 2017.

PARADE

or Public Augmented-Reality ADvertising Experience is a form of interactive advertisement. In our developed prototype, the user can interact with a car commercial in real time, using nothing but a smart phone with an internet connection and no additional software

By connecting to a website, the user is given complete control over the car's orientation with additional features such as zooming, switching car colors, turning of the headlights and opening doors! As a fun feature we have also added a minigame where you actually drive the car.

The project was developed at KTH in Stockholm, Sweden.
