PARADE

PARADE (Public Augmented-Reality ADvertising Experience) is a form of interactive advertisement. In our developed prototype, you are able to interact with a car commercial in real-time, using nothing but you phone and no additional software. By connecting to a website you are given complete control over the cars orientation with additional features such as zooming, switching lights, changing the color or opening doors. As a fun feature, we also added a minigame where you are able to drive the car. We used a free open-source car model and developed all additional graphics and software ourselves.

The project was done as part of the Advanced Graphics course at KTH (Royal Institute of Technology) in Stockholm, Sweden.

PlanBLOCKS

PlanBLOCKS is a virtual reality game that places you in the middle of an attacked village. Use your body motions to throw axes, block attacks with a shield or evade them and defeat incoming enemies. Achieve complete immersion without any handheld controlers using a VR headset and a body motion sensor.

The project was developed as part of the Advanced Graphics course at KTH and was showcased at ForskarFredag and Comicon in Stockholm, Sweden where it got positive feedback from people of all ages.

Survive VR

Survive VR is a simple survival virtual reality game, developed for the HTC Vive. The game places the player in a medium sized graveyard map. To survive the approaching enemy waves, the player uses both long and close range weapons, a bow and a sword. To make the game more interesting, checkpoints spawn randomly and allow the player to gain power-ups such as damage multipliers or frozen arrows.

The game also implements a simple gesture recognition, allowing the player to cast six different spells using hand motions

The project was developed for purposes of the Virtual reality course at EPFL, Switzerland.
